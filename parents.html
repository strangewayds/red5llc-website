Talk to Rosie & Community

How chat & community stay safe

Red 5 has two ways for kids to connect — the friendly Talk to Rosie companion and the Community. Both are built to be safe spaces, not open chat rooms.

  • Talk to Rosie is a guided helper. It offers kind, encouraging, age-appropriate responses and gently points kids to a trusted adult for anything serious. It is not a real-time chat with strangers.
  • Community is moderated. Posts pass through filters and human review, and children can't exchange personal contact details.
  • Report & block are one tap away. Anything that doesn't feel right can be flagged for a moderator instantly.
  • No open messaging with strangers. There are no private inboxes that let unknown adults reach your child.

Reminder: Talk to Rosie is a supportive feature, not a counselor or emergency service. See the family note below.

An important note for families

Red 5 is a supportive community and educational resource. It is not a replacement for professional therapy, medical care, or emergency support. If a child is in immediate danger, contact emergency services.

Clear boundaries

What kids can & can't do

Simple, predictable rules so you always know what your child is using.

Kids can

  • Watch curated Rosie videos and shows
  • Play games and use the Create Zone tools
  • Read along with stories and books
  • Earn badges and join monthly challenges
  • Share kind, friendly posts in the moderated community
  • Ask Talk to Rosie for encouragement

Kids can't

  • Share full names, addresses, schools or phone numbers
  • Message strangers privately or be contacted by unknown adults
  • Reach the open, unfiltered internet
  • See ads or behavioral ad targeting
  • Make purchases — only a parent can
  • Change account or safety settings

Parent FAQ

Common questions from grown-ups

Honest answers about safety, privacy and control.

Is Red 5 actually safe right now?

Red 5 is a prototype today. The safety measures on this page are our commitment for launch — dependable safety depends on trained human moderators and a secure backend, which are in place before any real child accounts go live. We won't overclaim production-grade safety until it's real.

Who can my child talk to?

Your child can post in a moderated community and use the Talk to Rosie companion. There is no open private messaging with strangers, and personal contact details can't be shared.

What information do you collect?

Only what's needed to create and protect an account, set up by a parent. We never sell children's personal data and we don't run behavioral advertising to kids.

How do I cancel or close the account?

Parents manage everything from the Parent Dashboard and can pause, cancel or close the account at any time, with no cancellation fees.

Is Talk to Rosie a counselor?

No. Talk to Rosie is a supportive, encouraging feature — it is not therapy, medical care, or emergency support. If a child is in immediate danger, contact emergency services.
